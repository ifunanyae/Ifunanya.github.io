--- layout: categories title: Categories --- # ITEC442 ## ACTIVITY 1- Digital Enterprise ### What is a fully digital enterprise? It is an organization that has integrated digital technology into all aspects of its operations, processes and functions. This transformation involves leveraging digital technologies such as cloud computing, big data analytics, artificial intelligence, internet of things (IoT) and automation to enhance efficiency, innovation and customer experience. Reference: https://theecmconsultant.com/digital-enterprise/ , chatgpt, lecture material. ### What are the cyber security challenges/ concerns with a fully digital enterprise? The complexity of IT environments. I.) Data breaches- There is a high risk of data breaches, due to the storage of large volumes of sensitive data. This is where the hackers come in. II.) Phishing attacks such as tricky emails that may be sent to employees to trick them into giving away company secrets. III.) Ransomware Attacks IV.) IoT vulnerabilities and smart gadgets, with devices like cameras or sensors, hackers can use this as vulnerable points to get into the company’s network. V.) cloud security and safety, if the cloud services where most businesses store their data on is not secure, their data might be at risk. VI.) Trusted people, sometimes company employees might misuse their access to sensitive information either accidentally or on purpose. References: https://www.anomali.com/blog/dealing-with-the-cybersecurity-challenges-of-digital-transformation , chatgpt, lecture material. ### What are the cyber security challenges for a bricks and mortar SME wanting to become a digital enterprise? I.) Limited money and people, most small businesses usually don’t have a lot of money or staff and buying the right tools to stay safe online can be tough. II.) Not knowing enough (Lack of Knowledge and expertise), people in the business might not know a lot about online safety III.) Data protection IV.) Phishing attacks V.) outdated systems- These systems and softwares might not have the latest safety updates, which makes them easy targets for cyber attacks. VI.) Third-party Risks- small and medium-sized enterprises often rely on third-party vendors for various services and if these vendors have weak cybersecurity, it can indirectly affect the SME’s security. VII.) Mobile securities.
